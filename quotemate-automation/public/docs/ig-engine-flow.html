IG Engine · How it works

HOW A TEXT BECOMES A PICTURE

A customer texts QuoteMate. Four engines later, they get a quote with a photo of their own room — with the new fittings already installed.

This page explains what happens between those two points, in plain English.

The pipeline

FOUR STOPS, START TO FINISH.

01

Customer message

A text, voice call, or photo upload. The raw request — usually messy, partial, conversational.

02

Intake Engine

Reads the message + photos. Turns it into a tidy record: what job, how many, what room, who, when.

03

Estimation Engine

Prices the job using the tradie's real price list. Builds three tiers — Good / Better / Best — with real products.

04

IG Engine

Edits the customer's room photo to show the finished install. Counts checked, products matched, retried until right.

The full flow

FROM MESSAGE TO IMAGE.

Each engine hands a tidier, more structured packet to the next. The IG Engine is the only one that produces an image — everything before it is text and data.

02 · Intake Engine

THE LISTENER.

Turns a messy human message into structured facts the rest of the system can rely on.

What comes in

  • SMS text or voice transcript.
  • Photos the customer uploaded.
  • Their phone number and name (if known).

What the AI is told

  • Pull out: what job, how many, what room, who, when.
  • Look at the photos and add what you see.
  • Decide: new install or replacing something old?
  • Decide: safe to quote, or send to a site inspection? Some jobs always need a visit — gas hot water, switchboard work, anything on the mains.
  • Tag the trade (electrical vs plumbing) from the job type.

What comes out

A tidy record with every field filled in:

{ job_type, count, room, replacing?, tenant, customer, photos, needs_inspection }

03 · Estimation Engine

THE PRICER.

Takes the structured job and builds a real quote with three tiers — using the tradie's actual price list, not made-up numbers.

What comes in

  • The structured record from the Intake Engine.
  • The tradie's price list (hourly rate, callout, markup).
  • A catalogue of real products — with prices and photos.

What the AI is told

  • You're a [electrical / plumbing] estimator in [NSW / QLD].
  • Use only prices from the tradie's price list. Never invent a number.
  • Build three tiers — Good, Better, Best. Same install, different product brand and finish.
  • If something doesn't fit cleanly, fall back to a $99 site inspection rather than guess.
  • A separate non-AI check verifies every price came from the price list. Quotes that fail get downgraded to a $99 inspection automatically.

What comes out

A three-tier quote where every line item is linked to a real product (including its photo):

{ good: {...}, better: {...}, best: {...} }

04 · IG Engine

THE ARTIST.

Takes everything we now know — the job, the count, the exact product — and edits the customer's photo to show it installed.

What comes in

  • The customer's room photo.
  • The exact product photo from the quote.
  • All the structured details: count, brand, room, customer name.

What the AI is told (~400 words)

  • Task. Edit the attached photo to show the install completed.
  • Spec. Exact count, brand, model, finish.
  • Must. Render exactly N items. Match the anchor product. Keep walls, floor, furniture, lighting identical. Photoreal, day-of-handover.
  • Must not. Wrong count. People, packaging, text overlays. Generic substitutes for the real product.
  • Placement. Ordinal list of where each item goes (e.g. "downlight 1: front-left of room…").

What comes out

The edited photo, uploaded to storage, ready to show the customer on their quote page.

The original photo's shape (16:9, 4:3 etc.) is preserved — no awkward cropping.

Accuracy safety net

THREE CHECKS ON EVERY RENDER.

Image AI gets things wrong. The IG Engine assumes it will, and catches them.

Two-pass for replacements

When the job is replacing an old fitting, the engine first does a removal pass — strips the old one out — then installs the new one. Stops the AI leaving both in the picture.

Independent Claude judge

A different AI (Claude) reviews the rendered image. Counts items, checks the product matches, confirms the old fitting is gone. Different model = different blind spots.

Retry with feedback

If the judge fails the render, the engine re-renders with the exact defect named — "you drew 7 but we asked for 6" — and tries again. Up to twice.

A real example

SIX DOWNLIGHTS IN SARAH'S LOUNGE.

  1. 01 · Customer. Sarah texts: "hi can i get a quote for 6 downlights in my lounge, replacing the old ones, here's a photo"
  2. 02 · Intake Engine. Reads the text + photo. Produces: { job_type: "downlights", count: 6, room: "lounge", is_new_install: false, photos: ["sarah-lounge.jpg"], needs_inspection: false, trade: "electrical" }
  3. 03 · Estimation Engine. Builds three tiers from the electrician's price list. Each tier is "6 × downlights" — same install, different brand. Picks the matching product photo from the catalogue for each tier.
  4. 04 · IG Engine. Edits Sarah's lounge photo. Removes the old downlights (pass 1). Installs 6 of the chosen brand in an even grid (pass 2). Keeps the walls, sofa, flooring identical.
  5. 04a · Judge. Claude reviews: counts 6 ✓, product matches ✓, old ones gone ✓. Pass.
  6. 05 · Sarah sees. A finished-install photo of her own lounge, with the quote underneath. Decision becomes obvious.
